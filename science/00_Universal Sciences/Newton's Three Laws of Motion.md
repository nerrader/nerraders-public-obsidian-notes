> [!INFO] Introduction
> 
> The laws of motion, developed by Isaac Newton, describes the fundamental relationship between an object's motion, and the forces acting upon it.

### **1. Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia):** 

An object at rest remains at rest, and an object in motion remains in motion at a constant speed and in a straight line unless acted upon by an unbalanced external force. This principle explains that objects naturally resist changes to their state of motion, a property known as inertia. 

The equation for this law of motion is as follows:

$\sum F= 0$
$\sum F$ = sum of forces (N)

---

### **2. Newton's Second Law (Law of Force and Acceleration):** 
The force acting on an object is equal to the mass of that object multiplied by its acceleration. This law establishes that acceleration is directly proportional to the net force applied and inversely proportional to the object's mass, with both force and acceleration being [[00_SI Units#Scalar and Vector Quantities|vector quantities]]. 

This law can also be expressed mathematically as:

$\sum F = ma$
$\sum F$ = sum of forces (N)
m = mass ([[00_SI Units|kg]])
a = acceleration $(m/s^2)$

---

### **3. Newton's Third Law (Law of Action and Reaction):** 
Whenever one object exerts a force on a second object, the second object exerts an equal and opposite force on the first. This means that forces always occur in pairs, If Object A pushes Object B, Object B pushes back on Object A with the exact same magnitude but in the opposite direction.

$F_{A \to B} = -F_{B \to A}$

> This can also be said as:

$\sum F_A = \sum F_R$ 
(sum of force of action = sum of force of reaction)

> Note: The negative sign indicates the opposite direction.

>[!abstract] **Definition** 
>The **Electromagnetic Force** is one of the four fundamental forces of nature. It acts between particles that possess **electric charge**. It is a long-range force (infinite in theory) that is mediated by the exchange of massless particles called **photons**. It acts upon all particles possessing an electric charge. It is unified into two manifestations: **Electrostatics** (stationary charges) and **Magnetism** (moving charges).

Electromagnetism is a pretty abstract force, so let's just start from the more basic principles of it first.
## [[Electricity]]

> [!abstract] **Atomic Neutrality and Ions**
> - **Neutral State:** An atom where the number of protons ($p^+$) equals the number of electrons ($e^-$). The net charge is zero.
>- **Ionization:** The process of adding or removing electrons. **Cation**s are **positively charged** ions and **Anion**s are **negatively charged** ions.
> - **Electrostatic Interaction:** Even neutral atoms interact via electromagnetism because their internal charges attract and repel each other at close ranges.

Everything is made out of atoms, atoms are usually electrically neutral (protons = electrons). However, if you ionize them, something happens. The cations are **repelling their own kind**, while **attracting the opposite kind** (anions).

This is **Electrostatic Force**. Charged particles exert a straight line push or pull on each other. It is the mechanism that holds atoms together in molecules and is the cause of everyday phenomena like static electricity (e.g., hair sticking to a balloon).

#### Coulomb's Law
This law defines the electrostatic force ($F$) between two point charges ($q_1, q_2$) separated by a certain distance ($r$).

$$F = k_e \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}$$

**$k_e$**: Coulomb's constant ($\approx 8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$).

## Electromagnetism with Electric and Magnetic Fields

- **Electric Fields:** Generated by stationary electric charges (Coulomb's Law).
- **Magnetic Fields:** Generated by the motion of electric charges (current).

A changing electric field creates a magnetic field, and a changing magnetic field creates an electric field (Electromagnetic Induction).

#### Lorentz Force Law
Describes the total force ($F$) on a point charge ($q$) moving with velocity ($v$) through an electric field ($E$) and a magnetic field ($B$).

$$F = q(E + v \times B)$$

> [!ABSTRACT] Definition
> 
> Gravity ($F_g$) is a universal force that pulls two objects with mass towards each other. Gravity is one of the **[[00_Fundamental Forces MOC|Four Fundamental Forces of Nature]]**.

### The Mechanics of Attraction

- **Direction:** Always directed toward the **center of mass** (the core).
- **Measurement:** Distance ($r$) between two objects is always measured from **center-to-center**, not from the surfaces.
- The more mass, and the less distance (from the core), the more powerful gravity is.

This means that every thing in the Universe, including the microorganisms, are technically attracting everything else. It's just that the gravity is not powerful enough for us to 'feel' it.
### Gravitational Fields

The "Field" is the region of space where an object experiences a gravitational pull.

- **Field Strength Symbol:** $g$
- **Unit:** Newtons per Kilogram ($\text{N/kg}$)
- **Earth's Surface Constant:** $\approx 10 \text{ N/kg}$ (Technical value: $9.78 \text{ N/kg}$)

To calculate the pull of gravity on a specific object (its **Weight**):

$\text{Weight (N)} = \text{mass (kg)} \times \text{gravitational strength (g)}$

> [!WARNING]- Mass vs Weight
> Do not get these two mixed up, they are both very different things
> 
> - **Mass** is a constant,
> - **Weight** is a variable that changes depending on $g$
> 
> For more information, go here: [[Mass and Weight]]

> [!INFO]+ Formula of Universal Gravitation
For objects in space (like the Earth and Sun), we use Newton's Universal Law:
$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$
> - $G$ = Gravitational Constant $\approx 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$
> - $m$ = Masses of the two objects
>- $r$ = Distance between centers
>
>Notice how the Gravitational Constant (G) is incredibly small, even in the negatives, this tells us that gravity is an extremely weak force compared to the other fundamental forces.
>
>This formula also shows that a shorter distance matters significantly more than a heavier mass, as in this formula, the distance is squared.

> [!WARNING]- "G" vs "g"
Do not confuse these two in your calculations:
>- **$G$:** It is the Gravitational **Constant** of the Universe.
>- **$g$:** The "Gravitational Field Strength" is used specifically at a planet's surface.

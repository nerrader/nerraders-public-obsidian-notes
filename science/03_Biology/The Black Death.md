
The plague is a severe infectious disease caused by bacterium that spreads to humans through bites of infected fleas that live on rodents, it is a very infectious and dangerous disease.]The beginning of the plague can be traced to its origin in Central Asia, where the bacterium naturally lived in wild rodent populations. As trade routes such as the Silk Road expanded, infected fleas traveled on animals, goods, and caravans which slowly brought the bacteria into contact with human settlements. It would later develop into major outbreaks like the black death due to it spreading to many regions..

Before the development of modern science and microbiology, people relied on superstition and observation to explain it. The plague was believed to be caused by the alignment of planets, which was thought to disrupt natural balance and poison the air. Another widely accepted idea was the theory of miasma which suggest that diseases were spread through foul smelling air produced by decaying mater. These misunderstandings let to ineffective and sometimes harmful responses. It was not until the 19th century when the true cause was discovered.

Yersinia pestis is the bacterium that caused the plague, including the Black Death, and it mainly exists in small animals like rodents and the fleas that feed on them. One reason it is so dangerous is because it can avoid the body's natural defenses and produce harmful effects that lead to severe illness.

Plague spread due to human activity

Plague symptoms can cause lungs to die, and cause some limbs to turn black and be dysfunctional, basically amputating them.

Impact on society: massive population decline, changes in economy and labor, and social and cultural transformation.
